Oro Blancos

What is an oro blanco?

An oro blanco is a citrus made from crossing a grapefruit and a pomelo. It has bright yellow skin and thick, bitter white rind protecting the sweet and tender flesh inside, which is a pale yellow color.

The name "oro blanco" is Spanish for "white gold." However, oro blancos were actually first created in the laboratory at UC Riverside in a series of citrus experiments beginning in 1958. Today, they can mostly be found in California at farmer's markets and specialty grocers.
